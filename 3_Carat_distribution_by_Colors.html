Diamond Carat Distribution by Colors

The width of the box plot corresponds to the number of diamonds in the respective category. J is the least desirable where as D is the most desirable in terms of Color.

From the chart, we observe that I and J, which are the worst Colors for diamonds, have the least number of diamonds. Also, the Carats in these categories have the widest range of distribution with relatively higher number of outliers and with the highest medians. The diamond with the highest Carat count (of approx. 5) lies in J.

As we move from J to D, the median Carat count decreases, with D, which is the best Color of diamond, having the least median Carat count. We can possibly conclude that better Color in diamonds are associated with a lower Carat count.
