Diamond Carat Distribution by Cuts

The width of the box plot corresponds to the number of diamonds in the respective category. ‘Fair’ is the least desirable whereas ‘Ideal’ is the most desirable in terms of Cut.

From the chart, we can observe that the ‘Ideal’ Cut, which is the most desirable, has the lowest median Carat along with the narrowest band of 25th – 75th interquartile range implying more uniformity in terms of Carat count for this category.

On contrary, ‘Fair’, the least desirable Cut, has the highest median Carat. It is also the category with the maximum outliers. The diamond with the highest Carat count (of approx. 5) lies in this category.

Finally, the ‘Premium’ Cuts also has a comparably high median of Carat count with a relatively wide band of 25th – 75th interquartile range and relatively high outliers signifying inconsistency in this bucket.
