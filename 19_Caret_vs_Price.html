Diamond Exploration


The objective of this exploration is to gain a deeper understanding of the properties of diamonds. The dataset in consideration has data for approximately 54,000 diamonds. Three important parameters tend to describe the overall value and worth of a diamond. In this analysis, we look at diamonds from the perspective of each of these properties. These include:
1. Cut – measures the quality of the diamond cut (Fair, Good, Very Good, Premium, Ideal)
2. Color – measured through alphabets from D (best) to J (worst)
3. Clarity – captures the clearness of diamond; measured through 3-chr code (I1 (worst), SI2, SI1, VS2, VS1, VVS2, VVS1, IF (best))
The outcome is measured in terms of Price and Carat (i.e., weight of the diamond).

Some other measured aspects of diamonds:
4. x – length in mm (0 -- 10.74); y – width in mm (0 -- 58.9); z – depth in mm (0 -- 31.8)
5. Table width and z Depth as defined in the Data page

Diamond Carat vs Price


The Carat vs Price distribution depicts a positive relationship between the two parameters. A higher Carat would typically command a higher price.

Also, we notice that most of diamonds are clustered towards lower Carat and Price range. Finally, the diamond with highest Carat (of approx. 5) does not have the highest price.
